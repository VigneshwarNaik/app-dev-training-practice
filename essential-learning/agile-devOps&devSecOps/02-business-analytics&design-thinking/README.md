# Business Analytics

## 1. Business Analytics Introduction

Business Analytics is the process of collecting, analyzing, and using data to make better business decisions.

It helps organizations understand their performance, solve problems, and improve future results.

### Example

A supermarket can analyze sales data to find which products are selling the most.

---

# 2. Business Analytics Overview

Business Analytics helps businesses:

* Understand customer behavior
* Improve decision making
* Increase profits
* Reduce costs
* Predict future trends

### Main Steps

1. Collect Data
2. Analyze Data
3. Find Insights
4. Make Decisions
5. Improve Business Performance

---

# 3. Business Analytics Architectural Concepts

Business Analytics Architecture shows how data moves through a system.

### Components

### Data Sources

Data comes from:

* Sales records
* Customer databases
* Websites
* Applications

### Data Storage

Data is stored in databases or data warehouses.

### Data Analysis

Tools analyze the data and generate useful information.

### Reporting

Results are displayed using reports, charts, and dashboards.

### Simple Flow

Data Sources → Data Storage → Data Analysis → Reports & Dashboards

---

# 4. Business Analytics Techniques

Business Analytics uses different techniques to analyze data.

### Descriptive Analytics

Answers: What happened?

Example:

* Monthly sales report

### Diagnostic Analytics

Answers: Why did it happen?

Example:

* Finding reasons for sales decline

### Predictive Analytics

Answers: What may happen in the future?

Example:

* Predicting next month's sales

### Prescriptive Analytics

Answers: What should be done?

Example:

* Suggesting marketing strategies

---

# 5. Balanced Scorecard Overview

A Balanced Scorecard is a tool used to measure business performance.

It helps organizations track goals and improve performance.

### Four Perspectives

1. Financial Perspective

   * Profit
   * Revenue

2. Customer Perspective

   * Customer satisfaction
   * Customer retention

3. Internal Process Perspective

   * Efficiency
   * Quality

4. Learning and Growth Perspective

   * Employee skills
   * Innovation

### Benefits

* Better planning
* Improved performance
* Clear business goals

---

# 6. Management Dashboards

A Management Dashboard is a visual display of important business information.

It shows data using:

* Charts
* Graphs
* Tables
* KPIs (Key Performance Indicators)

### Example

A sales dashboard may show:

* Total Sales
* Monthly Revenue
* Top Products
* Customer Growth

### Benefits

* Quick decision making
* Easy monitoring
* Better business control

---

# 7. Design Thinking

Design Thinking is a problem-solving approach that focuses on understanding user needs and creating innovative solutions.

### Steps in Design Thinking

1. Empathize

   * Understand user needs

2. Define

   * Identify the problem

3. Ideate

   * Generate ideas

4. Prototype

   * Create a sample solution

5. Test

   * Check if the solution works

### Example

If students find an app difficult to use:

* Understand their problems
* Find possible solutions
* Create a prototype
* Test and improve it

### Benefits

* Better user experience
* Creative solutions
* Improved products

---

# Conclusion

Business Analytics helps organizations make better decisions using data. Tools such as Balanced Scorecards, Dashboards, Analytics Techniques, and Design Thinking help businesses improve performance, understand customers, and achieve their goals.
