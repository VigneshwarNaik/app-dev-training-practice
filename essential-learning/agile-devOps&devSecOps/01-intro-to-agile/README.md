# Agile Introduction

## What is Agile?

Agile is a way of developing software in small steps instead of building the whole project at one time.

The team develops a small part, tests it, gets feedback, and then improves it.

---

## Simple Example

Imagine you are building a house.

### Traditional Method

* Build the entire house first.
* Show it to the customer at the end.
* If the customer wants changes, it is difficult.

### Agile Method

* Build one room first.
* Show it to the customer.
* Get feedback.
* Make changes if needed.
* Then build the next room.

This makes the customer happier and reduces mistakes.

---

## Why Use Agile?

* Work is completed faster.
* Changes can be made easily.
* Customers can give feedback regularly.
* Team members work together better.
* Better quality software is produced.

---

## Agile Process

1. Plan the work.
2. Develop a small part.
3. Test it.
4. Show it to the customer.
5. Get feedback.
6. Improve it.
7. Repeat the process.

---

## Main Idea of Agile

"Develop small, test early, improve continuously."

---

## Advantages of Agile

* Fast delivery
* Easy to handle changes
* Better teamwork
* Customer satisfaction
* Continuous improvement

---

# Agile Methodologies

## What are Agile Methodologies?

Agile Methodologies are different methods used to develop software in a flexible and efficient way. They focus on teamwork, customer feedback, and delivering software in small steps.

Instead of completing the entire project at once, Agile methodologies divide the work into smaller parts and improve the product continuously.

---

## Types of Agile Methodologies

### 1. Scrum

Scrum is the most popular Agile methodology.

**Features:**

* Work is divided into short periods called **Sprints**.
* Each sprint usually lasts 2 to 4 weeks.
* Team meetings are held regularly to discuss progress.

**Benefits:**

* Faster delivery
* Better teamwork
* Easy tracking of work

---

### 2. Kanban

Kanban is a visual method for managing tasks.

**Features:**

* Tasks are displayed on a board.
* Work moves through stages such as To Do, In Progress, and Done.
* Helps track project progress easily.

**Benefits:**

* Better workflow management
* Increased productivity

---

### 3. Extreme Programming (XP)

XP focuses on improving software quality and customer satisfaction.

**Features:**

* Continuous testing
* Frequent releases
* Close customer involvement

**Benefits:**

* High-quality software
* Fewer errors

---

### 4. Lean Development

Lean Development focuses on reducing waste and improving efficiency.

**Features:**

* Remove unnecessary work
* Deliver value quickly
* Continuous improvement

**Benefits:**

* Faster development
* Reduced costs

---

## Advantages of Agile Methodologies

* Faster software delivery
* Easy to handle changes
* Better communication among team members
* Improved product quality
* Higher customer satisfaction

---

## Conclusion

Agile Methodologies are software development approaches that help teams work efficiently and deliver high-quality software. Popular Agile methodologies include Scrum, Kanban, Extreme Programming (XP), and Lean Development.

Agile is a simple and flexible software development method. It helps teams build software step by step, get feedback quickly, and make improvements throughout the project.
