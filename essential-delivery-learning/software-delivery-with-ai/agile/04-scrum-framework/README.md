# Module 4 – Scrum Framework

## 📌 Overview

This module introduces **Scrum**, one of the most popular frameworks in Agile. Scrum helps teams develop software in small iterations called **Sprints**, enabling continuous feedback and improvement.

---

## 🎯 Learning Outcomes

At the end of this module, you will be able to:

* Understand Scrum cycle and hierarchy
* Relate to Scrum roles
* Understand Scrum artifacts
* Learn estimation techniques
* Understand Scrum events

---

# 1. What is Scrum?

**Scrum** is an Agile framework used to develop products in **small iterations** called **Sprints**.

Instead of building the entire product at once, Scrum delivers software incrementally.

> **Build → Review → Improve → Repeat**

---

# 2. Scrum Cycle

The Scrum cycle repeats continuously.

```text
Product Backlog
      ↓
Sprint Planning
      ↓
Sprint (1–4 Weeks)
      ↓
Daily Scrum
      ↓
Sprint Review
      ↓
Sprint Retrospective
      ↓
Next Sprint
```

Each sprint delivers a working product increment.

---

Features needed:

* Payment

Build:

```text

```

---

## Sprint 3

Build:


# 4. Scrum Roles

Scrum has **three main roles**.

## Product Owner

Represents customers and business needs.

### Responsibilities

* Prioritize features
* Maintain Product Backlog
* Define project goals

Example:

> "Build the payment feature first."

---

## Scrum Master

Ensures the team follows Scrum practices.

### Responsibilities

* Remove obstacles
* Facilitate meetings
* Support the team

Example:

If developers face technical issues, the Scrum Master helps resolve them.

---

## Development Team

Builds and delivers the product.

Includes:

* Developers
* Testers
* Designers

---

# 5. Scrum Artifacts

Artifacts are important documents used in Scrum.

---

## Product Backlog

A list of all project requirements.

Example:

```text
✓ Login
✓ Search Buses
✓ Payment
✓ Notifications
```

Think of it as a **master to-do list**.

---

## Sprint Backlog

Features selected for the current sprint.

Example:

```text
Sprint 1:
✓ User Registration
✓ Login
```

---

## Increment

The working software delivered after a sprint.

Example:

```text
After Sprint 1:
Users can login successfully.
```

---

# 6. Scrum Events

Scrum defines **five events**.

---

## Sprint

A time-boxed iteration lasting **1–4 weeks**.

---

## Sprint Planning

The team decides:

* What to build?
* How to build it?

Output:

```text
Sprint Backlog
```

---

## Daily Scrum

A **15-minute daily meeting**.

Each member answers:

1. What did I do yesterday?
2. What will I do today?
3. Are there any blockers?

---

## Sprint Review

The team demonstrates the product to stakeholders and collects feedback.

Example:

Customer says:

> "Please add UPI payment."

---

## Sprint Retrospective

The team discusses:

* What went well?
* What can improve?

Purpose:

Continuous improvement.

---

# 7. Estimation Techniques

Estimation helps teams predict effort.

---

## Story Points

Story points measure complexity.

| Feature | Story Points |
| ------- | ------------ |
| Login   | 2            |
| Search  | 5            |
| Payment | 8            |

Higher story points indicate more complex work.

---

## Planning Poker

A collaborative estimation technique.

Team members choose story points independently:

```text
1, 2, 3, 5, 8, 13
```

Then discuss differences and agree on a final estimate.

---

# 8. Scrum Hierarchy

```text
Product Owner
       ↓
Scrum Master
       ↓
Development Team
```

All roles collaborate to deliver value.

✅ Easy adaptation to changes

# 🔑 Key Takeaways

* Scrum is an Agile framework.
* Work is completed in **Sprints**.
* Scrum has **three roles**:

  * Product Owner
  * Scrum Master
  * Development Team
* Scrum uses **artifacts**:

  * Product Backlog
  * Sprint Backlog
  * Increment
* Scrum events help teams plan, review, and improve.
